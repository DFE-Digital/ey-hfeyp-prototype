Recap and further reading

What you've learned

You've learned that:

  • the brain is made up of the:
    • cerebellum
    • occipital lobes
    • parietal lobes
    • frontal lobes
    • temporal lobes
    • brain stem
  • babies and children need stimulation in order to help their brains develop
  • the stage of brain development between birth and 2 is called the sensorimotor stage

Put what you’ve learned into practice

As an early years practitioner you can encourage and support brain development by:

  • understanding the factors that may have impacted on a child’s brain development on their journey so far
  • providing a supportive environment with opportunities for children to practise activities, encounter new experiences and try things out for themselves
