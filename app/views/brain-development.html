Supporting physical development in the early years

On this course you’ll learn about the importance of physical activity for young children’s overall development.

This course, written by paediatric research scientist Dr Ben Roberts and nursery manager Kate Smith, describes how a child develops physically from birth to age 5 and gives advice and tips for early years educators.

Learning outcomes:

On completing this course, you will be able to:

  • describe the body systems and their relationship to moving and learning
  • explain the importance of movement skills to the overall health, wellbeing and development of young children
  • identify the appropriate physical and movement activities for each age and stage of development from 0 to 5 years
  • the importance of secure relationships in the early years
  • explore how parents and practitioners can work together to suppose the physical development of all children

The experts

Dr Ben Roberts

Dr Ben Roberts is a lecturer in Early Childhood at the University of Halifax. His research focuses on the importance of physical development in a child’s overall development and learning.

Kate Smith

Kate Smith has worked in early years education since 1998. She currently manages 5 nurseries and has previously worked as a childminder.
