Case study: Sarah plays peekaboo

Childminder Chris is playing with 6 month old Sarah. Chris is covering a teddy with a blanket. When the teddy is covered, Sarah immediately pulls off the blanket to find it and giggles.



According to Piaget, around what age do babies usually develop their understanding of object permanence?

That's not right.

Babies usually understand that an object they can no longer see still exists around 9 to 10 months. But all children develop at different rates. Some children may learn this earlier or later.
