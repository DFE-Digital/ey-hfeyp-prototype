Setting good habits

It’s essential to set good eating habits in children right from the start. If they learn this at an early age, they are more likely to keep these good habits as they grow up.

Good nutrition is important for children aged 5 and under to ensure they:

  • get the right amount of energy (calories) and nutrients they need while they are growing rapidly
  • don’t consume too much energy (calories), which may cause them to become overweight or obese
Introducing a balanced diet and good eating habits at a young age encourages children to eat a variety of foods which contain essential nutrients. These are especially important during this time of early growth and development.

This is a crucial time to help to reduce health inequalities throughout a child’s life. Too many children do not get the healthy foods they need to thrive. Your setting can influence these children’s food choices helping them develop a good relationship with food.
