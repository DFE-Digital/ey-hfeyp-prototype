Before birth and young babies

Research suggests that emotional development begins even before a baby is born. By the sixth month of pregnancy, babiesâ€™ hearing is nearly fully formed and they are becoming more aware of their environment in the womb.

At birth, a baby will communicate and show their feelings by crying. At first, they will only smile as a reflex, but by 6 to 8 weeks smiling becomes a controlled response to show happiness and pleasure.

In your setting

To help with their emotional development, make sure that you give babies lots of:

  • eye contact
  • positive facial expressions
  • reassurance
  • cuddles
  • comfort when crying
