4 years old

At around 4 years old, children would rather play with others than alone. They will be making friendships and forming bonds with their peers. At this age, children’s cooperation skills will be improving and they will be more aware of the feelings of others.

Children’s play will also be more creative, with an increase in purposeful imaginative play.

In your setting

You should provide an environment and resources that allows children to role play significant figures and events in their lives. This will help children to explore and express their thoughts and feelings and navigate relationships.

Some children will now talk more about what they like and what they are interested in. They will also have more of their own opinions and beliefs.

In your setting

You should support children to explore their interests in a child led way. For example, if you have a child with an interest in dinosaurs, you could have some toy dinosaurs available for them to play with.

Some children may continue to have difficulty regulating their emotions. They will need lots of opportunities to discuss and explore feelings. These activities will also be particularly useful for children with autism.

Around this age, children will be more aware of the world around them.

In your setting

You should encourage childrens’ curiosity by:

  • providing a wide variety of books, materials and experiences, covering topics such as cultures, religions, how things work and the natural world
  • planning trips
  • inviting visitors to the setting
  • offering open ended materials that can be used in many ways, such as sand and arts and crafts equipment

Reflection point

Reflect on what you have read about the social and emotional development of children aged 4.

Think about your work with 4 year olds (where applicable).

Consider the following questions:

  • What opportunities do you offer to help 4 year olds express their likes, opinions and beliefs?
  • How do you encourage 4 year olds to be curious about the world around them?
  • What else could you do to support their curiosity and self-expression?
